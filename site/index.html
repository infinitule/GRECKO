GRECKO

a counter-swarm decision engine that spends to win, not to react
When a swarm of cheap drones attacks, the hard problem isn't hitting them — it's not going bankrupt doing it. GRECKO decides which interceptor to commit to which threat, under whose authority, to stop the most damage for the least money.

⚠ Scope

Simulation, research, and C2-software only. No fire-control, no RF/jamming/waveform design, no weapon or hardware control. Effectors are parameter sets (cost, kill probability). A CI gate enforces this boundary on every commit.

What you just watched

Both defenses usually stop a similar number of drones — the magazine is the same size. The difference is two decisions GRECKO makes that legacy doctrine doesn't:

Ration the magazine With only 9 interceptors against 11 threats, something leaks. Legacy fires at whatever is closest and runs dry — often leaking a high-value drone it never got to. GRECKO spends its 9 rounds on the 9 highest-value threats and deliberately lets the two cheapest leak. Same shots fired, far less damage through.
Match effector to threat A $90k kinetic round to kill a $1,200 quadrotor is a losing trade even on a hit. When a low-value threat can be stopped by an $800 effector, GRECKO uses it — and reserves the expensive kinetic rounds for the targets that actually need them.

Neither decision is magic. Both are economics — and they're exactly what a saturating swarm is designed to punish you for getting wrong.

The 8–22% is the allocator's contribution in an apples-to-apples comparison (same effectors on both sides), measured across seeds and adversary tactics. Cherry-picked single raids — like the demo — show larger gaps because they also include effector selection; we don't headline those. Intercept rate is interceptor-count-limited, so it stays comparable between doctrines; the win is on cost and damage prevented, which is the metric a swarm actually attacks.

What's actually built — six parts, one decision

Every module serves the single question: which interceptor, against which threat, at what cost, under whose authorization.

Run it yourself — every command here actually works

pip install -e .              # installs the `grecko` CLI

grecko version                # confirm install + scope
grecko eval --seeds 6         # Monte Carlo cost-exchange, prints JSON numbers
grecko verify                 # the 4 architectural invariant checks
grecko serve                  # live C2 decision-engine bridge (ws://:8765)
make test                     # full acceptance suite (305 tests)

The operator console in viz/ connects to grecko serve over WebSocket — that's the real human-on-the-loop interface, where you see the air picture and authorize each engagement. Deployable as two containers with make docker-up. See the repository and docs/DEPLOYMENT.md.
