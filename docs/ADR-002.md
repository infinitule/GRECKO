# ADR-002: GNN-first association, two-pass track competition, bearing-only as update-only

**Status:** Accepted  
**Phase:** P2 — Fusion & multi-target tracking

## Context

Fusion turns the P1 report streams (cartesian + bearing-only, asynchronous,
cluttered) into the unified air picture every downstream layer consumes.
Saturation behaviour is the point: the design must stay real-time and
high-purity at the 40-target acceptance level and degrade measurably — not
catastrophically — beyond it.

## Decisions

### Per-track Kalman filter, constant-velocity, IMM-ready

State `[x, y, vx, vy]`, discretised white-acceleration process noise, Joseph-form
updates for numerical stability. The filter is a self-contained object exposing
`predict`/`update`/`mahalanobis2` — an IMM bank can wrap several instances behind
the same surface without touching the tracker. Bearing-only measurements are
handled as an EKF update linearised about the predicted state relative to the
observing sensor.

### GNN first, JPDA later, behind an `Associator` interface

Global Nearest Neighbor = Hungarian assignment on chi-square-gated Mahalanobis
distance (gates: 9.21 / 2-dof cartesian, 6.63 / 1-dof bearing). GNN is optimal
for the one-report-per-target regime, easy to audit, and O(n³) worst case —
the benchmark shows it real-time through ~80 targets. JPDA slots in as a second
`Associator` implementation when dense-clutter scenarios demand it.

### Two-pass association (the bug this phase actually fixed)

With a 99% gate, ~1% of true reports fall outside their track's gate and would
initiate a duplicate tentative track. In a single global assignment pass that
duplicate then *competes* with the real track and steals measurements, holding
covariance inflated indefinitely (observed: RMS stuck at raw-noise level).
Fix: confirmed/coasted tracks associate first; tentative tracks only see
leftover reports. Outlier-spawned duplicates starve and die under M-of-N.

### Scan-aligned hit/miss bookkeeping

The tracker is ticked at 50 Hz but sensors report at 2–10 Hz. Hits/misses are
counted only on report-carrying cycles; report-free ticks do prediction and
time-based coast/drop only. Without this, empty ticks starve every track
before confirmation (observed: zero confirmed tracks).

### Lifecycle

tentative → confirmed (M-of-N, 3-of-5) → coasted (>1.5 s without update) →
dropped (>6 s). Coasted tracks recover to confirmed on a single hit.

### Bearing-only reports never initiate tracks

A single bearing fixes no position. Bearing-only reports (EO/IR bearing mode,
ACOUSTIC) only *update* existing tracks via the EKF bearing update. Multi-sensor
bearing triangulation for initiation is deferred (v2 backlog).

## Rejected alternatives

- **JPDA from the start:** soft assignment helps in dense clutter but is harder
  to audit and unnecessary at baseline false-alarm rates. The interface keeps
  the door open. Rejected for v1.
- **Track merging instead of two-pass association:** merging fixes duplicates
  after they corrupt both estimates; priority association prevents the
  corruption. Rejected.
- **Global single RNG-rate fusion (only update at sensor epochs):** loses the
  clean 50 Hz tick contract with the world loop. Rejected.

## Acceptance evidence

- Single-target RMS 7–10 m vs 25 m raw noise; velocity within 5 m/s.
- Crossing targets keep dominant-truth identity through the crossing.
- Zero confirmed tracks from clutter over 30 s at baseline rates.
- Saturation benchmark (2 radars @ 4 Hz, ring ingress):

| targets | confirmed | purity | RT factor |
|---|---|---|---|
| 10 | 10 | 1.000 | 34.9× |
| 20 | 20 | 1.000 | 13.3× |
| 40 | 40 | **1.000** | **4.3×** |
| 60 | 60 | 0.994 | 2.1× |
| 80 | 81 | 0.935 | 1.3× |
| 100 | 102 | 0.870 | 0.9× |

40-target criterion (>90% purity at real-time) met with margin; saturation knee
is at ~80–100 targets, which is the honest engineering answer the benchmark
exists to expose.
