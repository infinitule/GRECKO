# ADR-010: P-EVAL — Monte Carlo cost-exchange evaluation

**Status:** Accepted
**Phase:** PX — Monte Carlo + headline figure

## Context

AEGISNET's central claim is that the EconomicMDP allocator (Pillar A) is more
cost-effective than a greedy baseline without sacrificing unacceptable amounts
of intercept capability. PX quantifies that claim across the adversarial attack
formations discovered by the co-evolution league (PL) and under the nominal
sensor model established by PS.

The deliverable is a structured headline figure: the mean cost-exchange ratio
(CER) for each allocator, the improvement fraction, and a per-scenario
breakdown — all deterministic and regressable via CI.

**Scope:** simulation and analysis only. Effector types are parameter sets
(cost, Pk). No hardware control, RF design, or weapon integration.

## Decisions

### Defense spend from physical intercept events, not alloc-cycle bookkeeping

The EconomicMDP re-optimises the assignment matrix every 0.5 s (25 ticks) via
Hungarian algorithm. As interceptor and threat positions evolve, the optimal
pairing changes and each change would trigger a `magazine.expend()` call — up
to 30+ expends per episode for 3 physical interceptors. This makes the
alloc-cycle magazine an unreliable cost estimator.

The correct physical cost is: for each `INTERCEPT` event in the world event
log, look up the consuming interceptor's `effector_type` and charge its
catalogue cost. This gives 0–3 charges per episode (one per consumed
interceptor) and is grounded in what actually happened in the world.

With this model: EconomicMDP CER ≈ 24.8, GreedyMyopic CER ≈ 32.0 — a 22.5%
reduction in defense cost per intercepted threat value.

### Headline metric: cost-exchange ratio (CER)

CER = defense_spend_USD / intercepted_threat_value_USD.

A lower CER means the defense is more cost-efficient. The nominal cost
parameters ($1,000 per commercial quadrotor UAS threat, effector costs from
the catalogue: kinetic $90K, net capture $3K) produce CERs between 3–96
depending on which interceptors physically engage.

The EconomicMDP advantage comes from directing the expensive kinetic
interceptor to the highest-priority target and net-capture drones to
lower-priority tracks, rather than the greedy dispatch which sends whichever
interceptor is geometrically closest.

### Paired allocator comparison across PL-discovered scenarios

Each scenario runs with BOTH allocators at the SAME seed so the outcome
difference is attributable to the allocator, not to stochastic variation.
Scenarios include the default bridge formation and policies from the PL tactic
library, ensuring the evaluation covers adversarial attacks the defense has
never trained against.

### The intercept-rate / CER trade-off is explicit

Greedy: higher intercept rate, higher CER (engages everything feasible).
EconomicMDP: lower intercept rate, lower CER (holds fire on low-value targets
to preserve magazine for higher-value later arrivals).

This trade-off is surfaced in the headline figure as `intercept_rate_delta` and
is NOT hidden behind a single scalar.

### Test configuration uses a fast league for CI speed

The full evaluation uses a 4gen×6pop league (≈160 s). The CI test uses a
2gen×4pop league (≈23 s) with 3 seeds and 1 PL tactic, completing in ≈191 s
via the module-scope fixture shared across all test classes.

## Measured results (3 seeds × 2 scenarios × 2 allocators)

| Allocator    | Mean CER | Mean IR  | Mean spend / episode |
|-------------|----------|----------|---------------------|
| EconomicMDP | 24.75    | 0.33     | $74,000             |
| GreedyMyopic | 32.00   | 0.38     | $96,000             |

CER improvement: **22.5%**. IR delta: −5pp (Greedy engages slightly more).

Breakdown by scenario:
- `default`: Eco CER 28.4 vs Grd 32.0 (12% improvement; Grd IR 38% vs Eco 31%)
- `pl_0`: Eco CER 21.1 vs Grd 32.0 (34% improvement; similar IR)

The largest CER improvement appears on the PL-discovered adversarial formation
(pl_0), where EconomicMDP's threat-value awareness lets it ignore feint tracks
and concentrate magazine on the main axis.

## Rejected alternatives

- **Magazine-round counting as cost:** double-counts re-targeting decisions;
  produces CERs of 200–380 (physically meaningless). Rejected.
- **Single-episode evaluation:** insufficient coverage of stochastic sensing
  and tracker variation. Monte Carlo required. Rejected for the headline figure.
- **Leaker rate as headline:** combines Blue's magazine efficiency with Red's
  tactic strength, obscuring the allocator's contribution. CER is
  allocator-specific. Rejected.
- **Full mutual co-evolution now:** Blue retraining requires the PX loop to
  confirm the cost-exchange improvement, which it now does. Mutual
  co-evolution is the natural next step after PX.

## Acceptance evidence

- 27/27 tests pass in `sim/tests/test_px_eval.py` (259 cumulative, 1 skip).
- Headline figure structurally complete: both allocators, CER, IR delta (A).
- EconomicMDP CER ≤ Greedy CER across all tested seeds and scenarios (B, C).
- Per-scenario breakdown covers every scenario in the study (D).
- CERs in physically interpretable range [0, 1000] (E).
- Results deterministic: same config → identical headline figures (verified).
